Why Do I Need Electrolytes?

Electrolytes are essential for hydration, and proper nerve functioning.

A more detailed explanation on their functions can be found in this article , and this blog post

What Are Good Electrolyte Ratios?

A typical composition looks something like this:

  • Sodium: 1000 mg
  • Potassium: 200 - 500 mg
  • Magnesium: 25 - 75 mg

For reference, you can check the ratios used by some well respected brands, like LMNT, SolSalts, and Redmond ReLyte.

Additionally, there is a great article on the LMNT website, in which they explain in detail why they use the composition they use, based on research.

Why Would I Mix My Own Electrolytes?

There are two main reasons to do so.

#1 In many countries, there are no well formualted electrolytes supplements available, but it is possible to get salt, potassium, and magnesium.

#2 It's generally much cheaper. As an example, 30 servings of the popular Electrolyte brand LMNT cost you $45 or $39 with subscription. Using the preset example above here, you could make 600 servings before you run out of the potassium supplement (just fill in 600 servings, and you see you'll use almost 500 grams of potassium).

Your total cost would be $19.95 for the potassium, $33.95 for the Magnesium, and $12 for salt, together $65.90.

For the same 30 servings, that would be $3.30. This is without my applied discounts, and not taking into account that you would still have a lot Magnesium left over for the next batch.

How Much Sodium Is There In Salt?

Salt is roughly 40% Sodium, and 60% Chloride.

 Which Salt Should I Choose?

Use one that is minimally processed, and doesn't have Iodine added to it. The finer the grain, the easier it will dissolve. Salts like Celtic Sea Salt or Pink Himalayan Salt contain additional trace minerals, but the amounts are so small they are not significant. Just use what is affordable and available to you.
